Section 01

Mindset: You Earned the Right to Promote

Let's get this out of the way first. If you are reading this, you already provide real value to your community. You share tips, strategies, event breakdowns, entertainment, and guidance that helps people play smarter and enjoy their games more. That's real work.

Promoting Packsify is not "selling out." It is the opposite. You are pointing your community toward a service that genuinely saves them money, keeps their accounts safe, and gives them a better experience. You know this because you use it yourself.

Every major content creator works with partners. That's how the creator economy works. The difference is whether you promote something you believe in or something you don't. You believe in this. So own it.

The Reframe
Think of it this way: if a friend asks you where to get the best deal on packs, you would tell them about Packsify without hesitating. Promoting on stream is just doing that at scale. You are not asking for a favor. You are sharing something useful and earning from the value you bring. Both things can be true at the same time.

Yes, some people will comment "you only care about money" or "stop promoting stuff." That happens to every creator with any partner. Here is the reality: the people who complain loudest are almost never the ones who spend. Your spenders, the people who actually buy packs, are thankful you showed them a better way. Focus on them.

The Trust Layer: How to Educate and Build Real Belief

This section might be the most important one in the entire playbook. The difference between a creator who drives 5 orders and one who drives 500 comes down to one thing: how well your audience understands why Packsify works.

A generic "use my code and save money" does not cut it. That's what every affiliate in every niche says. It tells nobody anything specific. Your audience needs to understand the same thing you understand. If every one of your viewers saw Packsify the way you see it, they would all use it for every purchase. Your job is to close that understanding gap.

Speak to Everyone as if They Are a Stranger

Here is a counterintuitive insight: even people who have heard you mention Packsify ten times may not fully understand how it works or why it is safe. We strongly suggest you explain the same thing repeatedly, even to the same audience. Each time you explain, some people hear a detail they missed before. Some people are watching for the first time. Some people need to hear it five times before they act. Never assume your audience "already knows." Explain like you are talking to a stranger, every single time.

Section 03

Platform Strategy: YouTube + Kick + Discord

The smartest creators do not put all their effort into one platform. They build a system where each platform does a specific job.

YouTube = Discovery and Traffic

YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine. When someone searches "Evony KvK guide" or "Whiteout Survival tips," your video can show up for months or even years after you publish it. That is passive discovery no other platform can match. YouTube is where strangers find you.

YouTube is also strict with rules. Content can get flagged, demonetized, or removed without much warning. So think of YouTube as your storefront window. It is clean, polished, and brings people in. But your real business happens elsewhere.

Discord = Community and Retention

Once someone finds your YouTube, the next step is getting them into your Discord server. This is your home base. No algorithm changes, no platform bans, no risk of losing your audience overnight. Discord is where casual viewers become community members, and community members become loyal customers.

Your Discord is also where you can be more direct about Packsify. Pinned messages, a dedicated channel, mod responses with your code: all of this works naturally in Discord without any platform risk.

Kick = Livestreaming and Freedom

Kick is the platform built for livestreaming, and it gives you something the others don't: complete freedom in how you talk about your partners. No content restrictions on gaming purchases. No vague community guidelines that change every month. You can promote openly, show your orders on screen, and talk about Packsify as much as you want without worrying about strikes.

The Funnel in One Sentence
YouTube brings the traffic. Discord holds the community. Kick is where you build deep relationships and drive action. All three feed each other.

This means you can test Kick with zero risk while keeping your existing YouTube streams exactly as they are.
Section 05

Smart Platform Transition: Adding Kick Step by Step

You don't need to abandon YouTube. And if you currently have 100-200 viewers on YouTube streams, you do not need to rush this. The goal is to gradually add Kick as a second home for livestreaming while keeping YouTube for videos and discovery.

Phase 1: Test the Waters (Week 1-4)

Start by multistreaming to both YouTube and Kick at the same time. Use Restream.io or Streamlabs Desktop. This means you go live once, and both platforms receive your stream. Your YouTube audience sees nothing different. But now you are also building a presence on Kick.

During this phase, casually mention Kick on your YouTube stream: "By the way, I'm also live on Kick right now. If you want to check it out, link is in the description." Do not push hard. Just plant the seed.

Phase 2: Give Kick Something Special (Week 5-8)

Start doing one exclusive thing on Kick per week. Maybe a Q&A session, a giveaway stream, or a casual "just chatting" stream where you play and answer questions. Something that gives your community a reason to create a Kick account and follow you there.

Phase 3: Shift the Balance (Week 9+)

Once you have a small but active community on Kick, start defaulting your regular livestreams to Kick. YouTube becomes your home for edited videos, highlights, and Shorts. You can still multistream when it makes sense, but Kick becomes your primary live platform.

What to Tell Your Audience

Be honest: "I'm adding Kick because I earn more there, I have more freedom, and you can support me more directly. YouTube is not going anywhere for videos and guides. But for live streams, Kick is the better platform for both of us."

For Creators With 100-200 YouTube Viewers
You do not need thousands of viewers to make Kick worthwhile. Even 30-50 active viewers on Kick who subscribe and tip can match or beat what you earn from YouTube livestreams, especially if your audience is outside the US where YouTube ad rates are very low. Build slowly. The momentum will come from consistency, not from a single big move.

Show, Don't Speak: The Proof Moment

Showing is roughly 10x more effective than telling. When viewers see you actually use Packsify, trust goes through the roof. This is not just a tip. It is the core strategy.

Proof Moment Formats

The Live Walkthrough: Open Packsify on screen. Show the game selection. Show the pack options. Show yourself placing an order. "Look, I'm ordering right now. It's that simple. I'll show you when it arrives." Then 10-20 minutes later: "Order just came through. Done."

The Relationship Proof: "I just got off a call with Stefan, the CEO of Packsify. We were planning some cool stuff for the community." Or: "Sara from Packsify just messaged me to check in. That is the kind of partner they are. They actually care." This is not scripted promotion. This is you sharing your real experience.

The Support Comparison: Show a screenshot of a Packsify support chat where your question was answered in minutes. Then ask your audience: "When was the last time you got a response like that from [game] support?" Everyone knows the answer.

The Order History: Show your Packsify order history on screen. "Look at this. I've been using Packsify for months. Every order delivered. Every account safe."

The Savings Breakdown: "I just bought $100 worth of packs through Packsify and saved $X. That is X% savings. Over a year of spending, that adds up to hundreds of dollars."

The DM Proof Clip

Record one short video (30-60 seconds) of you showing a Packsify order. Save it. Now, every time someone DMs you asking "where do you buy packs?" or "is Packsify safe?", just drop the video. Show instead of explain. Your mods can do the same in Discord. One video, used hundreds of times.

Speaking Openly About the Team
Mentioning specific people by name (Stefan the CEO, Sara the COO, your account manager) creates a level of trust that no marketing can replicate. It tells your audience: "I know these people personally. I trust them. This is not some random website." Even small things like "I have a meeting with the Packsify team later today" or "They asked me what features would help my community" signal that this is a real, close partnership.
Section 08

Stream Setup: Making Packsify Visible

The most successful streamers always have their partner's branding visible on screen. For us, the challenge is that Packsify is not a game you play on screen. So we need to be intentional about visibility.

Persistent Overlay Banner

Add a small, clean banner to your stream overlay that stays visible at all times. It should include: the Packsify name or logo, your creator code, and either a short URL or QR code. Position it in a corner or along the bottom. Anyone who clips your stream or glances at the screen should see it.

Scene Transitions

Use your "Be Right Back," "Starting Soon," and "Stream Ending" screens to display your Packsify message more prominently. These are dead moments where viewers have nothing else to look at. Use them.

Chat Commands (Kick Setup Guide)

If you have never used Kick before, here is how to set up chat commands. You have three options:

Option 1: KickBot (recommended for beginners). Go to kickbot.app and create an account. Connect it to your Kick channel. Navigate to "Custom Commands" and create these:

  • !packs Response: "Save on packs with Packsify! Use code [YOUR CODE] at packsify.com. 5 years, zero bans. Your account stays safe."
  • !code Response: "My Packsify code is [YOUR CODE]. Use it at packsify.com to save on packs and support this channel."
  • !save Response: "Smart commanders save with Packsify. Code [YOUR CODE] at packsify.com"

Then type /mod @KickBot in your Kick chat to give the bot permission to post messages.

Option 2: Streamlabs Cloudbot. If you already use Streamlabs, enable Cloudbot for Kick in your dashboard. Go to Commands and add the same commands. Type /mod @StreamElements in your Kick chat.

Option 3: StreamElements. Similar to Streamlabs. Connect to Kick, add commands, mod the bot in chat.

Set a timed message to automatically post your Packsify message every 15-20 minutes. KickBot and Streamlabs both support this. This way, even if you forget to mention Packsify verbally, the chat reminder goes out automatically.

Positioning: Trusted Partner, Not Sponsor
In everything you do, position Packsify as your trusted partner, not as a sponsor. A sponsor pays you to read a script. A trusted partner is a service you personally use and recommend. Use language like "the team at Packsify," "my partners at Packsify," "the people I trust with my packs." Never say "my sponsor Packsify." This is more authentic and more effective.

Leverage Plays: Do More With Less Effort

The word "leverage" means getting more output from less input. Some of these things look hard when you first try them. But once you get into the flow and make them natural, they save massive time and multiply your results.

One Stream = Five Pieces of Content

  • 1 YouTube highlight video (8-15 minutes of the best moments)
  • 3 short clips for TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels
  • 1-2 Discord posts (screenshot, quote, highlight moment)

At least one of those clips should include a natural Packsify mention or screen share.

The Real-Time Clipping Strategy

You don't do the clipping yourself. Your mod/clipper watches the stream, captures the best moments, adds captions using AI tools like CapCut or Opus Clip, and posts to your channels while the stream is still live. Someone sees the clip on TikTok, gets curious, joins the stream. More viewers, more potential Packsify users. With AI tools today, this takes minutes per clip, not hours.

The Answer Library

Build a collection of pre-made responses: a 30-second video showing a Packsify order ("Is it safe?" answer), a text response for Discord ("How does Packsify work?"), a link to your best explanatory video. Your mods can use these instantly without needing to type custom responses every time.

The Leverage Mindset
Every time you do something, ask: "Can I use this more than once?" If you show a Packsify order on stream, can your mod clip that and use it to answer DMs? If you explain how Packsify works live, can that become a YouTube Short? Once you think this way, effort compounds instead of being spent once and forgotten.
Section 12

YouTube Channel Strategy: Main + Clips

Many successful creators run two separate YouTube channels, and there is a good reason for it.

Your Main Channel

This is your polished, edited content: strategy guides, event previews, tutorials, deep dives. These videos are searchable, evergreen, and bring in new viewers through YouTube's algorithm for months or years. This is your discovery engine.

Your Clips Channel

This is a separate channel (usually named "[Your Name] Clips" or "[Your Name] Live") where you post raw or lightly edited clips from your livestreams: funny moments, big plays, community highlights, Packsify mentions, and shorter casual content.

Why Separate Them?

YouTube's algorithm learns what your audience expects. If your main channel is known for polished 15-minute strategy guides, uploading a raw 2-minute stream clip confuses the algorithm and can hurt your main channel's performance. By keeping clips on a separate channel, you protect your main channel's algorithm profile while still getting the discovery benefits of frequent uploads. Your clips channel also becomes a great place for Shorts, which YouTube pushes heavily to new audiences.

How This Helps Packsify Promotion
Your clips channel is the perfect place for Packsify content: a 60-second clip of you showing an order, a short where you explain how Packsify works, a highlight of a giveaway moment. These clips are lightweight to produce (your mod/clipper handles them) and they reach new audiences through Shorts discovery. Each clip is another touchpoint that moves someone closer to their first Packsify order.

Handling Competitor Questions With Facts

People will ask "Why Packsify and not [other service]?" The answer is never to trash the competition. The answer is to share facts and let the comparison speak for itself.

Facts That Win Without Naming Names

"How long have they been operating?" Packsify has been running for 5 years across multiple games. Many competitors appeared in the last year. Track record matters when your account is on the line.

"Can you talk to a real person?" With Packsify, you chat with real humans who respond in minutes. Many competitors are fully automated. Some do not respond at all when issues come up.

"What do the reviews say?" Look at Trustpilot reviews for any top-up service. You will find stories of bans, lost money, and no support. Then look at Packsify's track record. The difference is clear.

"How transparent are they?" When a creator asks Packsify direct questions about their business model, they get direct answers. Multiple creators have reported that other services dodge questions, give vague responses, or refuse to explain how they operate.

"Are they a real company?" Packsify is a US-based company (Wyoming) with a real team, real offices, and a real CEO you can talk to. Many competitors are faceless operations with no public team, no company registration, and no accountability.

The Repetition Strategy for Differentiation
You do not need to mention competitors by name. Ever. Just keep repeating the facts about Packsify: "real humans, real fast," "5 years, zero bans," "US-based company," "they answered every question I had." If you say these things consistently, your audience will automatically compare that to whatever else they see. The facts do the selling. You just have to keep repeating them.

If you have never set up a Kick giveaway before, here is a simple approach:

Step 1: In KickBot, create a command called !enter. Set the response to: "[sender] is entered into today's giveaway! Use code [YOUR CODE] at packsify.com for bonus entries."

Step 2: At the start of your stream, announce: "Type !enter in chat to join today's giveaway. If you've also ordered through Packsify this week, screenshot your order in Discord for double entries."

Step 3: Use KickBot's built-in giveaway feature or manually pick a winner at the end of the stream.

You can also set up a !pack command that triggers a timed cooldown (e.g., once per 30 minutes per user) so viewers can ask about Packsify anytime and get your code + link automatically without flooding chat.

Section 16

Build Your Team: Mods, Clippers, and AI Tools

You don't need a professional team. You need 1-2 people who already watch your streams and want to help. The same person can be both a mod and a clipper.

Where to Find Them

Your best mods are people who already show up consistently. They are in your chat every stream. They already answer other viewers' questions. They care about your community. These people often volunteer naturally. If they don't, ask directly in Discord: "I'm looking for someone who wants to help moderate streams and clip highlights. If you're interested, send me a DM." Most people will do it for free at the start because they enjoy being part of the team. You can reward them later with Packsify credits, shoutouts, or other recognition. Be open about looking for help. People want to contribute.

What They Do

  • During stream: Moderate chat, drop !packs command when someone mentions buying, share the proof clip when someone asks "is it safe?", clip interesting moments
  • After stream: Export clips, add captions using AI tools (CapCut, Opus Clip, Descript), post to your Shorts/TikTok, update Discord with highlights

AI Tools That Make Clipping Easy

In 2026, editing clips is not hard anymore. Tools like CapCut auto-caption your clips in seconds. Opus Clip can automatically identify the best moments from a long stream and cut them into Shorts-ready clips. Descript lets you edit video by editing text. Your mod/clipper does not need editing skills. They need to know which tools to use and which moments to capture. That is it.

Section 17

Show Your Face: Why It Matters for Trust

This might be uncomfortable for some creators, but it is important: showing your face on camera is one of the single biggest trust accelerators you can use.

Think about it from your viewer's perspective. They are deciding whether to trust Packsify with their game account and their money. If they see a faceless stream with just gameplay and a voice, the trust bar is low. If they see your face reacting, talking, and personally vouching for Packsify, the trust bar goes way up.

Top creators across every niche show their face. It makes your content more engaging, more personal, and more memorable. When you show your face and say "I personally use Packsify and I trust them," it carries more weight than the same words from a faceless voiceover.

If you are not comfortable showing your face for every stream, start small: use a facecam for the first 10 minutes of your stream during the intro and Packsify mentions, then switch to gameplay-only for the rest. Even that small amount of face time makes a big difference.
